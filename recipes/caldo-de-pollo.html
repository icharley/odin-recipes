Caldo de Pollo

Description

Caldo de Pollo is a traditional Mexican chicken soup made with vegetables, herbs, and tender chicken, typically served hot and comforting.

Ingredients

  • five pounds of chicken leg quarters.
  • broth starts with two gallons of water
  • minced garlic, salt, garlic powder, and chicken bouillon
  • carrots, potatoes, zucchini, a chayote, and an onion
  • fresh cilantro

Steps

  1. In a large pot, combine the chicken leg quarters and water. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat.
  2. Skim off any foam that forms on the surface of the broth.
  3. Add minced garlic, salt, garlic powder, and chicken bouillon to the pot. Stir well.
  4. Reduce the heat to low and let the broth simmer for about 30 minutes.
  5. Add chopped carrots, potatoes, zucchini, chayote, and onion to the pot. Continue simmering until the vegetables are tender.
  6. Remove the chicken from the pot, shred it, and return it to the soup.
  7. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving. Enjoy your Caldo de Pollo!
